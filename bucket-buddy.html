BucketBuddy

Live Site

GitHub Repos

Have you always wanted to take a trip somewhere? Are you interested in trying out a new restaurant? BucketBuddy is an app that allows you manage items on your bucket list so you can actively pursue enjoying new adventures! The app allows you to add a new adventure with an image, type notes about where, when, or with whom you may plan to have this unique experience, and check off the experience when the adventure has been completed.

Process

This app was built during a group project during my Software Engineering Immersive course at General Assembly. As an all female group in a male dominated cohort, we set out to prove that our development abilities were on par with our design skills. We wanted our app to be unique and thoughtful, so we spend a good chunk of the first day brainstorming and sketching out ideas.





We quickly realized that we may not have time to implement authentication, so we switched gears and focused on creating the main user interface for the app. We set up our database and created an interface where our user could input items on their bucket list and create cards with an image for visual interest.

Front of Cards




While we were a little nervous to divide and conquor at first, we decided that we needed to split up and work on different Git branches so that two of us could work on polishing the CSS for the interface on the front side of the cards and create the functionality for "checking off" items and two of us could focus on creating the functionality for getting the cards to flip so users could add notes on the back.

Back of Cards




We were eventually able to get the cards to flip and succesfully merge our branches to the master. We had ideally hoped to have the ability for the user to flip select cards, but we ran out of time and had to compromise with the outcome of having all of the cards flip once the user chose to edit any one of the cards. Overall, we were proud of the concept we came up with and we were pleased with the look and feel of the app in the end.
